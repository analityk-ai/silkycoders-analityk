# Most Important Questions for Stakeholder Meetings

## Strategic & Alignment Questions

### Business Objectives
1. **What are the primary business objectives for this initiative?**
   - What success looks like
   - Key metrics and KPIs
   - Timeline expectations

2. **How does this align with organizational strategy?**
   - Alignment with long-term vision
   - Priority relative to other initiatives
   - Budget and resource constraints

3. **What are the main business drivers?**
   - Market opportunity or threat
   - Competitive landscape
   - Regulatory or compliance requirements

---

## Requirements & Scope Questions

### Functional Requirements
4. **What specific problems are we solving?**
   - Current pain points
   - Impact on business/users
   - Root causes vs. symptoms

5. **What are the key functional requirements?**
   - Must-haves vs. nice-to-haves
   - Priority order
   - User personas affected

6. **What are the non-functional requirements?**
   - Performance expectations
   - Scalability needs
   - Security and compliance requirements

### Scope & Scale
7. **What is the scope of this project?**
   - Geographic scope
   - User base size
   - Data volume
   - System dependencies

8. **Are there any existing constraints?**
   - Technology stack requirements
   - Legacy system integration needs
   - Third-party vendor dependencies

---

## Stakeholder & Impact Questions

### Stakeholder Landscape
9. **Who are all the key stakeholders?**
   - Direct and indirect beneficiaries
   - Decision-makers and influencers
   - Support functions (IT, Finance, Legal)

10. **What are each stakeholder's priorities?**
    - Different perspectives on success
    - Concerns and risks
    - Required involvement level

### Business Impact
11. **What are the expected benefits?**
    - Financial returns (ROI, cost savings)
    - Operational improvements
    - Customer/user satisfaction gains

12. **What are the potential risks and obstacles?**
    - Adoption challenges
    - Technical risks
    - Organizational change management needs

13. **What are the dependencies?**
    - Other projects or initiatives
    - Data availability
    - Skills and expertise required

---

## Decision & Timeline Questions

### Decision Authority
14. **Who has the authority to make decisions?**
    - Decision-making process
    - Escalation path for conflicts
    - Timeline for approvals

15. **What approval gates exist?**
    - Governance requirements
    - Stage gates or milestones
    - Required sign-offs

### Timeline & Resources
16. **What is the expected timeline?**
    - Target launch/completion date
    - Phase delivery approach
    - Critical milestones

17. **What resources are available?**
    - Budget allocation
    - Team capacity
    - Expertise and skills
    - Tools and infrastructure

---

## Implementation Questions

### Approach & Methodology
18. **What is the proposed approach?**
    - Incremental vs. big bang
    - Pilot testing approach
    - Rollout strategy

19. **How will success be measured?**
    - Success criteria definition
    - Measurement methodology
    - Reporting cadence

### Change & Adoption
20. **What change management is needed?**
    - Training requirements
    - Communication plan
    - User adoption strategy
    - Resistance management

---

## Follow-up Questions Template

- **"Can you tell me more about..."** (dig deeper on key points)
- **"What would happen if..."** (explore scenarios and risks)
- **"How will we know if..."** (validate success criteria)
- **"Who else should we involve..."** (identify missing stakeholders)
- **"What are you most concerned about..."** (surface hidden issues)

---

## Red Flag Questions (Ask if things seem unclear)

⚠️ **If requirements are vague:**
- "Can you provide a specific example?"
- "What does that look like in practice?"

⚠️ **If timelines seem unrealistic:**
- "What assumptions underpin this timeline?"
- "What happens if we need more time?"

⚠️ **If success criteria are unclear:**
- "How will we measure this?"
- "What's the threshold for success?"

⚠️ **If stakeholder alignment is missing:**
- "Are all stakeholders aligned on this?"
- "What are the different perspectives?"

---

## Notes for the Analyst

- **Listen more than you talk** - Ask questions, then listen actively
- **Dig for root causes** - Don't settle for surface-level answers
- **Document assumptions** - Explicitly state what you're assuming
- **Validate understanding** - Paraphrase back to confirm comprehension
- **Follow the thread** - Let conversations flow naturally while covering key areas
